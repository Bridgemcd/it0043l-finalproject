SDG 11: SUSTAINABLE CITIES AND COMMUNITIES

Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable

Why It Matters

This SDG strives to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. The rapid urbanization and population growth have forced people into living in slums and informal settlements. Only 50% of the world’s urban population had access to safe and reliable public transport in 2019.

Key aspects of SDG 11:

  • Inclusive and Resilient Cities
  • Sustainable Urbanization
  • Access to Basic Services and Housing
  • Sustainable Transportation
  • Protecting Cultural and Natural Heritage
  • Green Spaces and Public Areas

In the Philippines...

SDG 11 is a key area of focus. Various initiatives are at play for challenges regarding housing, transportation, and environmental sustainability. These initiatives include:

  • Comprehensive land-use and strengthening urban planning
  • Improving public transportation systems and expanding bike lanes and walkways
  • Addressing the issue of informal settlements
  • Managing toxic substances and hazardous waste
  • Enhancing disaster response readiness, including the Metro Manila Flood Management Project
